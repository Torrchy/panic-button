‚ö° Quick Techniques (No Video Needed)
Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)
  1. Breathe IN for 4 counts
  2. HOLD for 4 counts
  3. Breathe OUT for 4 counts
  4. HOLD for 4 counts
  5. Repeat for 3-5 minutes
Use when: Panic, anxiety, can't catch your breath, need immediate regulation
Physiological Sigh
  1. Quick inhale through nose
  2. Second quick inhale through nose (double inhale)
  3. LONG exhale through mouth
  4. Repeat 3-5 times
Use when: Need fast calm-down, before important conversation, acute stress
Butterfly Hug (Bilateral Stimulation)
  1. Cross your arms over your chest
  2. Place hands on opposite shoulders
  3. Tap alternating left-right, slow steady rhythm
  4. Do for 30 seconds to 5 minutes
  5. Notice what comes up, let it pass
Use when: Dissociated, triggered, need self-soothing, EMDR-like processing
Havening (Self-Soothing Touch)
  1. Stroke your arms from shoulder to elbow, slowly
  2. Or stroke your face gently (forehead to cheeks)
  3. Or rub your palms together then cup your face
  4. Slow, gentle, repetitive
  5. Do for 2-10 minutes
Use when: Need comfort, alone and scared, before sleep, after nightmare
Voo Breath (Vagal Toning)
  1. Take a deep breath in
  2. On exhale, make a low "vooooo" sound (feel vibration in chest)
  3. Let it be long and slow
  4. Repeat 5-10 times
Use when: Frozen, shut down, need to activate vagal nerve, feeling trapped
Shaking/TRE (Trauma Release)
  1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
  2. Bounce gently on your knees
  3. Let your whole body shake - hands, arms, torso
  4. Don't control it - let it happen
  5. Do for 1-5 minutes, then rest
Use when: Frozen, after trigger, activation needs discharge, body feels stuck
Cold Water/Ice
  1. Hold ice cubes in your hands OR
  2. Splash very cold water on your face OR
  3. Hold ice to back of neck OR
  4. Dunk face in ice water for 30 seconds
Use when: Dissociated, panic, SI urges, need immediate grounding, numbness
Progressive Muscle Relaxation
  1. Start with your feet - tense them hard for 5 seconds
  2. Release and notice the difference
  3. Move up: calves, thighs, stomach, chest, arms, shoulders, face
  4. Tense each area for 5 seconds, then release
  5. Full body takes 10-15 minutes
Use when: Can't sleep, body is tense, holding trauma, before bed
