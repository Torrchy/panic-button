Communication & People
üö´ Don't Call Samaritans
Why: You've learned this destabilizes you more. Talking to strangers about feelings when you're in crisis doesn't help you - it makes it worse. You're not broken for this. It's just not your thing.
Do this instead:
  • Email Anahi (you can write without talking)
  • Physical grounding: hot bath, ice, weighted blanket
  • Movement: walk Maya, run, draw
üö´ Don't Call Your Parents

  • Your women's group (30+ group you've built)

  • Maya (she won't trigger you)
üö´ Don't Try to Talk About Feelings When Crying
Why: You've said explicitly - talking about feelings while crying destabilizes you. It doesn't help. It makes it worse. You don't process verbally when you're that activated.
Do this instead:
  • Let yourself cry without narrating it
  • Physical comfort: blanket, Maya, hot bath
  • WRITE it down if you need to get it out
  • Talk about it later (in therapy, or after you've regulated)
üö´ Don't Isolate from Your Women's Group

  • Just showing up is enough
Coping Attempts That Backfire
üö´ Don't Force Yourself to "Be Productive"
Why: When you're flatlined (especially 5pm-8pm window), forcing productivity makes you feel worse. It's not laziness - it's a physiological state. Pushing through reinforces shame.
Do this instead:
  • Accept the 5pm-8pm flatline as a known pattern
  • Plan low-demand activities: reading chair, drawing, gentle movement
  • Productivity can wait until you're regulated
üö´ Don't Make Big Decisions
Why: When dissociated, flatlined, or in crisis, your decision-making is impaired. You might quit your job, break up with someone, or make major life changes you'll regret. SI thoughts are especially not decision-making states.
Do this instead:
  • Use the 15-minute rule (or 24-hour rule for big stuff)
  • Wait until you're regulated
  • Write down the decision urge, revisit later
üö´ Don't "Think Your Way Out"
Why: Dissociation, freeze, flatline - these are BODY states, not thinking problems. Trying to logic your way out keeps you stuck in your head when you need to drop into your body.
Do this instead:
  • Somatic exercises
  • Movement: walk, run, shake
  • Sensory input: ice, hot water, texture
  • The solution is physical, not cognitive
üö´ Don't Skip Eating (Even When Food Feels Like a Chore)
Why: You have suspected ARFID and eating already feels like work. When you're in crisis, skipping meals tanks your blood sugar and makes everything worse. Your body needs fuel even when your brain says no.
Do this instead:
  • Keep safe foods available: Arlo yogurt, fruit, easy proteins
  • Eat SOMETHING even if it's "not enough"
  • Small snacks count
  • Don't judge what you can manage to eat
